Face sketch Recognizer in Digital Forensics using Computer Vision

During my third year of undergraduate studies, I embarked on a mini project that involved The Face Sketch Recognizer project in Digital Forensics is an application that utilizes computer vision techniques to match facial sketches with corresponding digital images for forensic investigations. It aims to assist law enforcement agencies in identifying potential suspects based on hand-drawn facial sketches, improving the accuracy and efficiency of investigations.

The project involves the following key components and steps:
1.Data Collection:
Gathering a dataset of facial sketches created by forensic artists. Collecting a separate dataset of digital facial images from various sources (e.g., surveillance cameras, social media).
2.Preprocessing:
Preprocessing the facial sketches to enhance their quality, remove noise, and normalize the images. Preprocessing the digital facial images to adjust for lighting conditions, size, and orientation.
3.Feature Extraction:
Extracting facial features from both the sketches and digital images using computer vision algorithms. Commonly used feature extraction methods include Local Binary Patterns (LBP), Scale-Invariant Feature Transform (SIFT), or Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) for deep feature learning.
4.Matching and Recognition:
Implementing a matching algorithm to compare the extracted features from the sketches and digital images. Utilizing machine learning techniques, such as Support Vector Machines (SVM), k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN), or deep learning models like Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), to perform the matching and recognition tasks.
5.Evaluation and Validation:
Assessing the accuracy and performance of the face sketch recognition system using appropriate evaluation metrics (e.g., precision, recall, F1 score). Validating the system's performance using a separate testing dataset and cross-validation techniques.
6.User Interface:
Developing a user-friendly interface that allows investigators to upload facial sketches and retrieve potential matches from the digital image database. Incorporating visualization techniques to display the comparison results and highlighting potential matches. The Face Sketch Recognizer in Digital Forensics project has significant potential to aid forensic investigations by automating and expediting the process of identifying suspects based on hand-drawn sketches. It can enhance the capabilities of law enforcement agencies and help solve criminal cases more effectively.
